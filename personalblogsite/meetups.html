Colorado Open Source Operating System User Group

09 November 2017

Meetup Information

Colorado Open Source Operating System User Group: Android - You already have linux on your phone

People I Met

Vincent Buscarello Backend development at UCROO Gave me a brief overview/conceptual introduction to docker

Pete Silva Web Dev Instructor Galvanize Boulder

What I Learned

I learned that Android is a distro of Linux (basically Android is built on a kernal of Linux?). I'm sure I don't fully understand this, but it was cool to get introduced to it. Termux is an Android command line app that mimics Linux.

I learned a little bit about(basically introduced to vocab or concepts): Kotlin busybox/toybox tombstone logcat heard a little bit about sockets.

This was definitely the densest (and perhaps most interesting meetup I've been to yet). I just tried to follow along as best I could. I did try and brew install Android SKD components for fun (unsuccessfully, got through installing Java, but could not get android-sdk to install).

Questions I Have

Questions: What is Kotlin? How is it used? What is the integration with Javascript? This looks interesting and new. I have for the last ten years or so been a mac/iphone person, so it was cool/interesting to see a bunch of developers nerd out over Android backend.

Where do tech design and product design intersect?

08 November 2017

Meetup Information

Denver Tech & Design Community: Product Design in the Outdoor Industry

People I Met

Elizabeth Sasseman, Graphic and UX Design The Rolland Group Boulder

Laxman Gani , User/Customer Experience (UX/CX) & Product Management Expert at Builder Homesite

Paige Jensen, formerly of La Sportiva North America

What I Learned

The meetup was a panel discussion about the intersection of physical product design in the outdoor industry and digital product design. Panel members were:

  • Adam Hevenor, Product Manager at Pivotal, Inc.
  • Matt Maczuzak, co-founder and Managing Partner, Alchemy Bicycle Co.
  • Jedd Rose, Founder and President, Topo Designs
  • Eric Whewell, Senior Designer at Boa Technology
  • Moderated/facilitated by: Chuck Sullivan, Something Independent

 

As someone who has worked on the retail side of the outdoor industry for the last three years, and am now becoming a web developer this was an interesting panel discussion to listen to. I was particularly interested to hear what Adam Hevenor at Pivotal had to say, as it relates most to what I am doing right now, and also is the thing I'm least familiar with. One of the questions posed to all panelists was, "What would you like to be remembered for?." In response to this, Adam talked about open source projects being the one thing he does that has a chance at lasting ten to fifteeen years (an eternity in the tech world). He mentioned Linux as an open source project that has lasted forty years, and while he didn't think anything he worked on would last that long, it's a testament to the value and lasting impact of open source. He also talked about Pivotal being a very metrics driven place. It was cool to have some idea of what he meant when he talked about test driven development too. I was reminded that generally speaking there is not a ton of money in the outdoor industry, for most people in the industry they are there because they love it and they can make a living (but not a killing). It did feel super comfortable and familiar - maybe I'll end up back in the outdoor industry yet, who knows.

Questions I Have

What are some open source projects I would be interested in contributing to? Curious to explore this more. Am I interested in working as a developer back in the outdoor industry? Time will tell here, and I'll continue to investigate.

Code for Denver

30 October 2017

Meetup Information

Code For Denver: Project Night

People I Met

Tommasina Miller, Rails developer at Granicus

Fred Lintz, web development student in third quarter of 6 month program at University of Denver.

What I Learned

Code for Denver is a really awesome organization where volunteer come to work on projects for three hours every Monday night. It is a brigate of Code for America and the focus is on civically minded open source projects. It was cool to both learn about the organization, meet some people, and to see a little of their workflow - both development workflow and general organizational workflow. They take volunteers each week to handle next weeks tasks as far as meetups go. Each week new members are onboarded. As far as project organization they use Flowdock and Waffle.io. I got to see a little of re:Imagine Denver's project and folks working on it. This was a particularly interesting first meetup to go to, as developers were actively working on projects they were invested in, and all are encouraged to participate, as opposed to a talk or more of a social/networking meetup.

Questions I Have

Wow! There is so much I don't know. Looking forward to learning more and being able to contribute to this group or other groups like it. I would like to spend some time learning a little about ruby/ruby on rails, as I don't even know the basics.
